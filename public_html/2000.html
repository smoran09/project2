2000s

Black Friday

In Washington, D.C., Greenspan believed technology created a new economy. Between February and March 2000, interest rates increased. On April 14th 2000, Black Friday occurred, the NASDAQ fell, and the stock market collapsed. Following, after 18 months the September 11th attacks occurred.
  • NASDAQ lost 2/3rds of its value.
  • Henry Blodget was found guilty of security fraud and was banned for life from securities.
  • eBay and Amazon survived. Andy Grove, creator of Intel, believed that the bubble was good and allowed for new companies created to be durable.

Communications Uprising

There was a communications uprising. YouTube, Myspace, and Facebook were created afterwards.
  • Kevin Rose and Jay Adelson created Digg which was news decided by users in 2004.
  • Mark Zuckerberg created Facebook in 2004.
  • YouTube was created by Chad Hurley and bought by Google in 2006.

  • The code was written in June 1999, and by October 1999, Napster took over 75-80% of bandwidth of universities. It became popularly known as the fastest spreading software.
  • Rosen was a record label protector.

  • On May 3rd, 2000, King, Paterno, and the drummer from Metallica had an encounter with Napster. They wanted the users to be banned who were breaking copyrighting laws.
